Research Interest

My research focuses on urban governance, particularly on how local governments make choices regarding collaboration with third parties. I examine patterns and mechanisms of collaboration between local governments and other actors to understand how these arrangements shape service delivery and governance outcomes.

  • Research interests: urban governance; local governments; collaboration; service delivery; network analysis; and urban shrinkage.

Previous Research

  • Masters thesis (2018). A Study on the Reconceptualization of Sharing and a Case Analysis of the Sharing Economy (Korean Title: : )
    • Advisor: Dr. Tae Young Kim
    • This study pertains to collaboration among individuals in urban contexts. Since the 2010s, "sharing" has become a rhetorical device in marketing and public policy, carrying connotations of community. However, its multiple and ambiguous meanings often generate debate. This study clarifies the concept of sharing, develops an ideal-typical framework, and applies it to a case analysis of car-sharing in Seoul, drawing on literature from anthropology, sociology, and business. This study provides insights by distinguishing different dimensions of sharing, particularly behaviors (allocation and commoning practices) and states of possession and ownership, and examining their implications.
